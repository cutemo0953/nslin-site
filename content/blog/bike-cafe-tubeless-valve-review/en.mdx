All three aim to solve the same problem: sealant clogging and restricted airflow in traditional Presta valves. But the paths they take are very different.

## CoreCap's Design Edge: External Threading = Larger Bore

The Bike Cafe review identifies the fundamental reason CoreCap delivers superior airflow — thread placement.

Traditional Presta valves and most competitors (including Schwalbe CLIK) place their valve mechanism **inside** the stem, secured by internal threading. Air must pass through a narrow channel partially occupied by the valve structure.

CoreCap inverts this: the check-valve mechanism lives in the cap, secured by the stem's **external** threads. The stem interior is completely clear. The effective air channel diameter equals the full inner diameter of the valve stem.

This is why the reviewer found CoreCap airflow to be clearly superior not just to standard Presta, but also to Schwalbe's CLIK VALVE. A larger channel means higher volumetric flow rate — basic fluid dynamics.

## Pressure Gauge Compatibility: A Detail That Matters

An exchange in the review's comments section addressed a practical concern: does CoreCap work with pressure gauges? The author confirmed — **no issues at all with manometer readings**.

This matters more than it seems. Some oversized valve designs (like Muc-Off Big Bore, which uses Schrader-diameter ports) require different pump heads, potentially making existing Presta-compatible pressure gauges unusable. CoreCap maintains Presta compatibility — no new tools required.

## Schwalbe's "New Standard" Claim

Bike Cafe directly challenged Schwalbe's market positioning. Schwalbe bills CLIK VALVE as the next-generation replacement for Presta, but the reviewer called this claim exaggerated.

In practice, CLIK VALVE requires a proprietary adapter to work with existing pumps — adding a new piece to every rider's toolkit. This runs counter to the simplification promise.
